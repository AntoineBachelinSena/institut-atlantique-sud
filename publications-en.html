STRATEGIC RESEARCH NOTE

The Blue Guiana and the Blue Amazon

By Antoine Bachelin Sena

The founding note of the Institute's analytical framework, this document links two maritime spaces into a single Atlantic strategic continuum. On the French side, French Guiana's exclusive economic zone — roughly 350,000 km² — concentrates gold and mineral resources, green-hydrogen potential (the Hyguane project) and the Kourou space centre, in an environment marked by illegal gold mining and cross-border pressures. On the Brazilian side, the "Blue Amazon" covers 5.7 million km² and carries most of the country's foreign trade. The note examines converging interests between Paris and Brasília — maritime surveillance, gold traceability, ocean research, subsea cables — and proposes a shared reading of vulnerabilities and levers for cooperation. It is the doctrinal reference to which the Institute's other publications refer.

STRATEGIC STUDY

Sanae Takaichi's Deep-Sea Mining Strategy

By Antoine Bachelin Sena

This study analyses Japan's strategy for accessing subsea mineral resources under Sanae Takaichi's government: mapping of rare-earth-rich muds off Minamitori-shima, the role of public operators, research funding, and a cautious sequencing between scientific exploration and potential exploitation. It places this trajectory within the negotiations at the International Seabed Authority and rising environmental requirements. The study draws lessons for Brazil and the defence of the Blue Amazon — notably around the Rio Grande Rise and the PROAREA programme: the primacy of scientific knowledge, the construction of a robust institutional framework, and sovereign control of ocean data as prerequisites for any decision. A comparative reading for French and Brazilian decision-makers facing the same trade-offs between environmental precaution and security of supply.

ANALYSIS NOTE

Why the Blue Amazon Will Become Brazil's Energy Heart in the 21st Century

By Antoine Bachelin Sena

This note argues a simple thesis: Brazil's energy centre of gravity is shifting offshore. Pre-salt oil and gas production, exploratory opening of the Equatorial Margin, the announced rise of offshore wind and hydrogen, and the densification of subsea cables mean the "Blue Amazon" now concentrates most of the country's energy-security stakes. The note reviews these dynamics, the infrastructure and surveillance needs they create, and the Brazilian Navy's growing role in protecting these critical assets. It identifies footholds for European partners — technology, finance, environmental standards — and the implications for the France-Brazil relationship, from Kourou to Rio de Janeiro. A framing document for understanding why the sea is becoming Brazil's primary energy frontier of the century.

STRATEGIC PROPOSAL

FFEMC — Franco-European Fund for the Critical Minerals of Blue Guiana

By Antoine Bachelin Sena

This proposal details the architecture of a Franco-European Fund for the Critical Minerals of Blue Guiana (FFEMC): a target endowment of EUR 500 million to 1 billion, governance combining public and private actors, and a mandate centred on responsible exploration, gold traceability and the structuring of local processing value chains. Aligned with the European Critical Raw Materials Act and inspired by existing instruments such as Guyana's Natural Resource Fund, the FFEMC aims to reduce European supply dependencies while offering a transparent counter-model to the opaque financing that fuels the illegal gold economy of the Guiana Shield. The document sets out capitalisation scenarios, investment criteria, environmental and social safeguards, and an implementation roadmap articulated with the relevant French and European institutions.

POSITION NOTE

Critical Minerals: Position Note on the French Strategic Context

By Antoine Bachelin Sena

This position note takes stock of France's critical-minerals strategy and argues for the full integration of French Guiana within it. Drawing on recent milestones in the national debate — the Varin report, the creation of the DIAMMS mechanism, the reform of the mining code, and Senate report No. 135 on the Guiana Shield — it identifies the blind spots: incomplete geological knowledge of the territory, a demanding but poorly legible operating framework, and insufficient articulation between the national and European levels. The note sets out operational recommendations: completing the Guianese mining inventory, guaranteeing a high-standard environmental and social framework, strengthening gold traceability, and positioning French Guiana as France's contribution to the Critical Raw Materials Act. It is addressed to the administrations, parliamentarians and operators concerned with French mineral sovereignty.

MANIFESTO

Founding Manifesto of the Institut Atlantique Sud

By Institut Atlantique Sud

The founding text of the Institut Atlantique Sud, the manifesto sets out the starting diagnosis — critical European dependencies in strategic materials, an under-analysed South Atlantic space, French and Brazilian sovereignties facing the same pressures — and the conviction that follows: Blue Guiana and the Blue Amazon form a single strategic continuum that calls for a dedicated place of analysis. The document defines the Institute's mission (producing independent, documented and actionable analysis in support of France-Brazil-EU cooperation), its values (sovereignty, intellectual independence, geoeconomic pragmatism, cooperation), its thematic scope and its working method. It also states what the Institute is not: neither a lobby nor an influence firm, but a producer of strategic knowledge in open access. It is the reference document for understanding the Institute's raison d'être.
